David E. Osher, Ph.D.
Postdoctoral Researcher
Dept. of Psychological and Brain Sciences, Boston University
dosher@mit.edu
I recently received my Ph.D. in Computational Neuroscience under the mentorship of John Gabrieli at MIT. I am currently a postdoctoral associate with David Somers at Boston University.

The brain is a massively interconnected network, and these connections determine what a brain region can do. My research explores the relationship between connective structure and neural function through computational modeling. I have demonstrated that these models can predict age, risk of dyslexia, and even how an individual's brain will respond to a variety of stimuli.
